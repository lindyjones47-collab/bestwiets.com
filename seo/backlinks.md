# Backlink Acquisition Strategy — bestwiets.com

**Why this matters:** every other lever on this project is done or in progress. **Backlinks are now the rate-limiting factor for ranking.** Especially for head terms like "wiet kopen" and "cannabis online kaufen", competitors win on backlink profile depth, not on-page work.

**Reality:** cannabis is a hard vertical for backlinks. Most mainstream sites won't link to a cannabis shop. The realistic strategy is **cannabis-adjacent directories, advocacy/info sites, and original-content outreach to cannabis blogs and forums**.

---

## 1. Link types in priority order

### Tier 1 — Easy, low-risk volume (do all of these in the first 30 days)

**Cannabis & coffeeshop directories.** These exist specifically to list shops. Most accept free submissions or charge a small one-off fee. They are usually low-quality individually, but in aggregate they provide the directory floor that Google expects a legitimate cannabis business to have.

Where to find them:
- Search Google for `"coffeeshop directory" Netherlands`, `"cannabis directory" Germany`, `cannabis "shop listing" Belgium`
- Search `inurl:directory cannabis netherlands` and similar
- Check what competitors are listed in via Ahrefs free backlink checker (one-shot lookup of `wietstore.nl` or similar)

**Local business directories** (general, non-cannabis):
- Yelp NL/DE/BE
- Yellowpages equivalents per country
- Google Business Profile **if** the legal regime in your country supports a public business listing (check first; cannabis policies are strict)

### Tier 2 — Editorial / content-led (start month 2)

**Cannabis blogs and news sites.** These are the high-value links. Approach: don't ask for a link, offer something useful — a piece of content, a quote, data, a guest article.

Niches to target:
- Dutch cannabis lifestyle blogs
- German Cannabis-Nachrichten / cannabis-legalisation news
- Belgian harm-reduction / info portals
- European cannabis trade publications

Search prompts to find them:
- `cannabis blog Niederlande` / `cannabis blog Nederland`
- `cannabis news Belgique` / `cannabis nieuws België`
- `"guest post" cannabis`
- `"write for us" cannabis Europe`

### Tier 3 — Forum & community presence (ongoing, slow burn)

**Forums and community sites.** A well-placed answer in a relevant thread is worth several directory links. **Don't spam** — Google catches this fast in cannabis.

Approach:
- Find threads asking real questions ("waar kan ik veilig wiet bestellen online?", "bezahlen mit bitcoin shop deutschland").
- Answer the question substantively (5+ sentences of real information). Only link to your site **if it's the most relevant link for the question**.
- Aim for 2–3 of these per week, not 20.

Common forums in the niche: Reddit cannabis-related subs, Drugforum, GroteMarkt-style buyers' communities (vet each carefully — some are not appropriate to associate with).

### Tier 4 — Unlinked brand mentions (free, easy when present)

When your brand starts appearing organically in articles, comments, or reviews, some of those mentions won't include a link.

Find them:
- `"bestwiets" -site:bestwiets.com` (Google search)
- Mention.com / Brand24 (paid, but free trial)

Then: email the author, thank them for the mention, ask if they'd be willing to make it a link. Conversion rate is 20–40%. Free easy wins.

## 2. Concrete first-30-days target list

Aim for **15 new referring domains** in the first month. Mix:

| Type | Count | Effort |
|---|---|---|
| NL/DE/BE cannabis directories | 8–10 | 30 min total to submit |
| General local business directories | 3–4 | 15 min total |
| 1 editorial mention (blog, news) | 1–2 | 2–4 hr of outreach |
| Forum answers with link | 1–2 | 30 min each |

**Total time investment:** ~6 hours over 4 weeks. Not difficult, just requires consistency.

## 4. Anchor text strategy

**Don't optimise anchor text aggressively.** Google penalises sites whose backlink anchors are over-weighted with commercial keywords.

Healthy mix for the next 90 days:
- ~40% brand: "WietStore", "Bestwiets", "bestwiets.com"
- ~30% naked URL: "https://bestwiets.com/", "bestwiets.com"
- ~20% generic: "klik hier", "more info", "this site"
- ~10% partial commercial: "wiet online bestellen bij WietStore", "WietStore cannabis shop"

**Almost never** pure commercial: "wiet kopen", "cannabis online kaufen" as anchor — this is the signal that triggers manual review.

---

## 5. What NOT to do (will hurt rankings)

1. **Buy bulk PBN links / link packages.** Cannabis is heavily scrutinised. One Fiverr "1000 backlinks for $20" purchase can poison the domain for 6+ months.
2. **Mass-comment spam on cannabis blogs.** Universally seen as spam, often nofollowed anyway, and bad for brand.
3. **Reciprocal link schemes** ("you link to me, I link to you"). Easy for Google to detect at scale.
4. **Hide backlinks in footers of unrelated sites.** Common in shady SEO services. Quickly demoted.
5. **Anchor-text-stuffing exact match** of head keywords. See above.

If you get pitched any "guaranteed first-page rankings in 30 days" service for cannabis SEO: it's spammy backlinks. Decline.

## 6. Tracking

A simple Google Sheet works fine for now:

| Date | Domain | Type (directory / blog / forum) | Status (submitted / live / declined) | Link URL | DR (Ahrefs) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Once per month, run a free Ahrefs Backlink Checker on `bestwiets.com` and reconcile against your sheet. New referring domains showing up that you didn't intentionally build are usually either:
- Scraper sites (ignore)
- Real natural links from people who found you (these are signals you're getting visibility — note them)

## 7. Budget guidance

You can do this whole plan with **near-zero budget** if you do it yourself. If you outsource:
- **VA for directory submissions:** €100–€200 for the first 20 directories (one-off).
- **Freelance writer for guest posts:** €80–€150 per article. Worth it only if it lands on a domain with DR 30+.
- **SEO tool (Ahrefs Lite or SEMrush starter):** €100–€130/month if you want serious competitor backlink intel. Skip in months 1–2; reconsider month 3.

Spending more than €500/month on backlinks for the first quarter usually doesn't pay back. The bottleneck early is finding good link targets, not money.

---

## 8. Six-month vision (beyond the 90-day window)

If the first 90 days establish a steady cadence (1–2 directories + 1 outreach per week), by month 6 you should have:
- 50–80 referring domains
- A handful of links from domains with real authority (DR 40+)
- 1–3 pieces of original content republished or quoted in cannabis news
- Foundation to compete for head terms by month 9–12

If month 6 has 5 referring domains, the strategy isn't working — usually because outreach stopped. Restart cadence.
